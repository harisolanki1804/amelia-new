Full Mouth Rehabilitation

We Give You a Brand New Smile

Nearly all of us will experience dental issues like dental cavities, chipped teeth, bleeding gums or yellowish teeth at some point in our lives. These are simple issues and have straightforward solutions. However quite a few of us have more complicated dental concerns like multiple lost teeth, extensive wear of the teeth, misalignment of teeth, pain in the jaw joints etc. These complex problems leads to a variety of problems like inability to chew well, extreme sensitivity or continuous recurrent pain and can wreck havoc in function and appearance which ultimately leads to a loss of confidence and self-esteem.

Dentistry has advanced in leaps and bounds in the past decade and it is possible to give beautiful long-lasting solutions to patients who have multiple dental needs. This treatment modality is called as “Full Mouth Rehabilitation” and our dentist’s at Amelia Street Dental Clinic - Jeet and Vijaya Wagh have decades of experience in treating patients with complex dental needs.

Full-mouth rehabilitation (FMR – also called full-mouth reconstruction) is not a single dental treatment. Rather, it is a treatment modality which encompasses a variety of treatments and procedures performed over time which are aimed at getting you a new smile and keeping your mouth healthy. It requires a thorough treatment planning, meticulous execution at the same time keeping ones expectations realistic. It may also involve the contribution of different dental specialists. But be assured, we at Amelia Street Dental Clinic can give you a whole new smile with full mouth rehabilitation so that you will enjoy better oral health, will feel better and will even have a great attractive smile.

What is Full Mouth Rehabilitation?

Full mouth rehabilitation is our comprehensive, fully personalized process for restoring a mouth that requires multiple... types of treatments.

Getting full mouth rehabilitation may involve bridges and crowns, veneers, implants, root canals, braces, dentures, gum treatment and other treatments. It also involves thorough understanding and analysis of your occlusion and jaw position, jaw movement and an attempt to simulate your teeth and jaw position when you were a young adult. It involves regaining your lost “The vertical dimension” which is relative position of the teeth and jaw with respect to each other. Loss of vertical dimension is the consequence of multiple problems like extensive wear of teeth, loss of multiple teeth, cavities, gum problems etc.

Note that we also offer a process called a “smile makeover.” A smile makeover typically is what we recommend for milder problems or fewer issues and is more related to the front teeth. A full mouth rehabilitation is our more extensive but comprehensive option for those with multiple problems and more severe dental problems in front and back teeth.
During your consultation, we can let you know what the ideal treatment option is for you. Either way, you will be getting a customized plan tailored to your mouth and goals.

Are You a Candidate for Full Mouth Rehabilitation?

Full mouth rehabilitation may be right for you if:...

• You are missing many teeth.
• You have a lot of tooth decay.
• Many of your teeth are chipped, cracked or broken.
• You have multiple unsightly fillings, inlays, or other dental work.
• You have crooked teeth.
• Your bite is not aligned.
• You have or have had gum disease.
• You have extensive wear of teeth and teeth are sensitive due to teeth grinding or other non-carious dental pathologies.
• You have had dental trauma which has affected your teeth and jaws.
• You have discoloured or internally stained teeth.

How We Restore Your Mouth to a Picture of Health

The exact process of full mouth rehabilitation can vary quite a bit from patient to patient. It involves a comprehensive ... examination, medical and dental history, thorough e/oral and i/oral facial and dental analysis and meticulous treatment planning and execution. It takes extensive patient motivation and compliance as it often is a lengthy process.

1. Come in for your consult. We will take X-rays and give your mouth a full exam. You also will have a chance to tell us about your treatment goals. I/oral and E/oral photos and study models will be taken to analyse the problems
2. We discuss the dental findings and problems and offer different treatment options, the costs involved. After this, together, we will come up with a custom plan to rehabilitate your mouth.
3. We will work through the treatments you select on a schedule that fits your needs.

How Do You Maintain Your New Smile?

Full mouth rehabilitation is a gradual process. Throughout that process and afterwards, you can maintain your oral... health and a beautiful smile by:

• Brushing, flossing, and using mouthwash each day.
• Not smoking.
• Limiting how much sugar you eat.
• Coming in for regular exams and cleanings

Remember adequate and satisfactory maintenance is required by the patient by practicing good home care hygiene methods and habits. Patients have to be very proactive on this count for good long term success and outcomes.

The state-of-the-art materials and techniques we use to rehabilitate your mouth mean that the dental work we do for you can last many years with proper care.
