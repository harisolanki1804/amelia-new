Put a Stop to Dental Pain Now With Emergency Dental Care

Are your teeth or gums in intense pain? Are you worried about severe tooth decay or infection spreading due to an abscess?

You don’t need to live with dental or oral pain. If you take action now, you can prevent worsening symptoms or complications. Amelia Street Dental Clinic can provide you with immediate emergency dental treatment for swift pain relief.

What is Dental Emergency Care?

Usually when you need dental treatment, you schedule an appointment for some time in the future. But some dental problems ... require immediate attention. You may not have the luxury of waiting weeks or even days to see a dentist.

If you need help now, Amelia Street Dental Clinic can get you in right away. We are available for emergency appointments Monday through Friday between 8:30 am and 7:00 pm. There are two emergency slots available each day, one in the afternoon and one in the evening

Are You Experiencing a Dental Emergency?

Here are some common reasons for emergency dental appointments:...

• Your tooth was chipped or knocked out due to an accident or sports injury.
• One of your fillings broke, causing severe, acute nerve pain.
• Tooth decay has led to a severe toothache.
• You are suffering from acute gum disease.
• A cavity has gone untreated long enough to cause an abscess.
• Your wisdom teeth are impacted, causing intense pain.

Getting in for emergency treatment will not only give you relief, but also a chance to take care of the problem as non-invasively as possible.

What to Expect

1. When you call us for an emergency appointment, we book you for the next available slot that is convenient for you.
2. Our dental experts conduct a thorough examination.
3. We advise you of the findings and the cause of pain and explain your treatment options.
4. If appropriate, we provide emergency treatment at the same visit. In some instances, you may have to be prescribed antibiotics and pain killers for pain relief.
5. We provide you with any instructions necessary for your continuing care at home.
