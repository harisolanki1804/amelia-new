Geriatric Dentistry

Enjoy Beautiful Teeth and Healthy Gums in Your Senior Years.

Think that gum disease and lost teeth are an inevitable consequence of getting older? Think again.

Dentistry for Seniors has advanced tremendously since your parents were your age. XYZ Clinic can help you to keep your teeth and gums healthy and functional for the rest of your life.

What is Geriatric Dentistry?

Geriatric dentistry is simply what we call the dental services we perform for older adults or Seniors....

Our focus is on preserving and maximizing the appearance and function of your teeth, gums, and jaws. It is even more important here in Australia for the seniors to take care of their teeth, gums and oral health as statistical studies have shown that out adult population is going to live longer till around 94-95 years of age. If they are able to maintain most of the dentition then age-related problems of chewing well, digestion, appearance and smile will be well taken care of.

We also consider your oral health in the context of your overall health. The mouth can offer a window through which to detect other health issues for early treatment.

Benefits of Dental Care for Seniors

• Replace cracked or missing teeth, filling in gaps for a full set of teeth....
• Whiten teeth that have become yellowed or stained for a sparkling white smile.
• Maintain or restore full function so you can chew and enjoy the food, and speak with ease.
• Restore a more youthful appearance to your mouth and face.

What to Expect During Your Appointment

We believe that “minimum intervention dentistry” is generally the best course for senior patients. Our goal is to maximize your oral health and minimize invasiveness and expenditures.

When you book your appointment, we work to provide you with a time and day that will fit your scheduling needs. We even offer special care for patients in long-term aged care facilities.

XYZ Dental provides a soothing atmosphere for your appointments. Our compassionate staff are empathetic and proactive in offering education to our patients. We put an emphasis on prevention so you can spend less time in the dentist’s office and more time with your loved ones.

Our staff have a strong understanding of how specific dental problems may correlate with systematic medical conditions. We also know how medications that are common for seniors to take may impact oral health and dental treatment.

Through our comprehensive exams, we are able to individualize strategies for each of our senior patients. It is important for the seniors to consult with their general practitioner and dentist for effective management of their general and oral health.

How to Maintain Your Oral Health After Your Visit
Between appointments, you can maintain your oral health by:

• Flossing, brushing, and using mouthwash daily.
• Not smoking.
• Avoiding eating too much sugar.
• Speaking to your doctor about medications that may be causing dry mouth.
