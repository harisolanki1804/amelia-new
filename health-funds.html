Health Funds (Dental Insurance) and Rebates

Amelia St Dental accepts all health funds, (including but not limited to BUPA, Medibank, HBF, HCF, HIF, NIB, Qantas health, AHM, CBHS etc) and your rebates or entitlements can be claimed instantaneously through our HICAPS terminal. The amount that each health ... fund member gets on his rebate is entirely dependent on the level of cover one has with the health fund and the preferred provider status of the practice.

Each health fund member will have a maximum limit of entitlement per year or an annual limit or rebate per year to claim. The amount is decided on the level of extras cover (or ancillary cover) one has taken up as part of their cover. The claim period usually runs from 1st Jan till 31st Dec each year, hence it is imperative to use your entitlement before the 31st of December each year. It is not carried forward to the next year therefore it is prudent for health fund members to claim it before it is forfeited.

Most of the preventive and diagnostic services like exams, x-rays, scale and clean are covered with small gap payable. Rebate for treatment services like fillings, extractions etc is determined on an individual basis and by your level of cover. It is best to clarify with your health fund on your level of cover. Typically, those who have major dental cover will get more rebate as part of their cover.

It is always preferrable to get a quote on HICAPS, so you are aware of the rebate and the gap payment.

Please note: Gap payments or out of pocket expenses will be dependent on your level of cover.

Metropolitan Patients Dental Subsidy Scheme (MPDSS)

Amelia St Dental as a private dental practice participates in the MPDSS scheme. Under this agreement, patients who have a letter from the Dental Health Services of the Government of Western Australia can have treatment at subsidised costs. Typically, ... the patient must pay 25% or 50% of the total fees and the remaining fee is paid by the Government of Western Australia directly to us. The entitlement letter will clearly state regarding the percentage of subsidy.

One can get a general treatment authority form, or an emergency treatment form issued by the Dental Health Services. These forms are issued to eligible people who are on the public dental waiting list etc . The Dental Health Services of WA will contact the individuals directly and issue them the letter.

Child Dental Benefits Schedule (CDBS)

The Child Dental Benefits Schedule is run by Services Australia or Medicare. If you have a child between the ages of 2 and 17 who requires dental care, you may be able to cover dental treatments through the Child Dental Benefits Schedule (CDBS) under Medicare. ... This benefits schedule gives you access to more than $1,050 of dental coverage for every two-year period. General dental treatment services like Check-up, X-rays, extractions, fillings etc are covered under this scheme. Cosmetic dental work like braces or ceramic veneers are not covered.

Patients are encouraged to contact Medicare to know the eligibility of their children. Our trained front office staff can also help you find out if your children are eligible for CDBS.

For more information, please visit https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/child-dental-benefits-schedule

Amelia St Dental is a bulk billing practice for the Child Dental Benefits Schedule. It means that children can avail their CDBS claim without having to pay anything extra out of pocket
