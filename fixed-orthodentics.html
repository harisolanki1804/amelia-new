Fixed Orthodontics (Braces)

Align Your Teeth for a Breathtaking Smile

Crooked teeth? Unsightly gaps? Problems with your bite? Any of these can erode your confidence and make you hesitant to smile. Worse, they can make it hard to chew, and in some cases, may even contribute to issues with bruxism (teeth grinding), tooth wear, and jaw pain.

With fixed orthodontics, Amelia Street Dental Clinic can correct the issues with your bite, perfecting your smile and protecting your oral health.

What is Fixed Orthodontics?

Fixed orthodontics are better known simply as braces. They are appliances ... which are fixed on the surfaces of your teeth, and you wear them for a period of 18 to 24 months. Gentle pressure is applied through a system of metal wires. This pressure gradually moves your teeth into alignment.

Once your teeth are aligned, the braces are removed, and you get to delight in your new smile.

Are You a Candidate for Braces?

Traditional braces may be right for you if: ...

• You have an issue such as overbite, underbite, or crossbite that you need to correct.
• Your teeth are crooked or have gaps.
• You do not want to worry about putting in and taking out a removable appliance each day to eat and brush your teeth.

We do offer an alternative to traditional braces as well called as Clear Aligners or better known as Invisalign. This brand name product features a set of clear custom aligners that fit over your teeth instead of a system that uses metal wires

Benefits of Braces

• Straighten your teeth for a cosmetic boost to your smile....
• Correct bite problems so your teeth meet properly.
• Chew your food more efficiently to enhance digestion.
• Speak more clearly.
• Prevent complications of a poor bite such as bone erosion or jaw pain.
• Reduce grinding and tooth enamel loss.
• Present yourself with confidence. Enjoy smiling and laughing.

How Braces Work

1. We start out with a full oral exam, a set of X-rays and a set of Intra-oral ... and Extra-oral photos during your consultation.
2. Next, we create a plaster dental cast called as study model.
3. A thorough planning and analysis is done using 3-D software and then the treatment plan is formulated. It is customised for each patient. This is then discussed with patient / parents and their consent is obtained. then discussed.
4. During this visit, we fix your braces. This initial process can take anywhere from 1-2 hours. An initial gentle force wire is engaged into the braces.
5. On a periodic basis, you will need to come in for an adjustment and change of wires. This is a quicker process than the initial placement and the appointment duration is between 30-40 minutes. You may feel mild discomfort for a few hours after these appointments. An over-the-counter Nurofen can help with pain management.
6. In a matter of 18-24 months, you will find your teeth getting aligned and corrected.
7. Once your teeth are fully aligned and your perfect smile is achieved, we can remove your braces and issue your removable clear retainers. You can then enjoy the full benefits of straight teeth! This ends the active corrective phase of treatment, and the most important retention phase of your treatment starts. The retentive clear aligners must be worn for almost 24 hours for a period of 12 months to 24 months. They can be removed only while brushing and eating food.
8. A final set of Xrays and photographs are taken marking the end of treatment. The changes and improvements that have occurred since the start are compared and evaluated.
How long you will wear your braces depends on your individual situation, but 1-3 years is typical.

At what should you start braces treatment

Ideally, fixed braces should be initiated during the growing phase of the individual.... (Girls between 11 to 14 years and Boys between 12-15 years,) This optimizes the results as the bone supporting the teeth is soft and facilitates ease of tooth movement.

However, braces can be done for adults too. The only prerequisite is good oral hygiene with minimal decay, excellent bone and gum condition and a lot of self-motivation on part of the patient.

Types of Fixed braces
Contemporary orthodontic treatment can be done using

1. Metal braces: These are metallic commonly made of Chrome-Cobalt alloy. Precious metal or Gold alloy braces are also available.
2. Ceramic braces: These are tooth coloured. The wires used are also tooth coloured and thus offer the advantage of being less visible to the naked eye.
3. Lingual braces: These are metallic braces fixed on the back side of the teeth., making them almost impossible to see. They are in close approximation to the tongue and therefore take a longer time to adjust. These patients will also tend to get more ulcers and brushing, and cleaning is cumbersome and takes a longer time.

The results achieved with any method is the same and the type selected will be based on individual’s needs.

Speech can be hampered initially with braces resulting in a lisp. It takes a couple of weeks to get adjusted to this and is not a long-lasting effect. After a small adjustment period, this is self-corrected.

How to Maintain Braces

You will need to make some minor changes to your routines while wearing braces.... Here is how to maintain them:

• Continue to brush and floss. In fact, you will want to spend a little extra time on each tooth. You can use a floss threader to get around your braces.
• Rinsing out your mouth can help you get rid of debris.
• Certain foods do not go well with braces. Examples include popcorn, nuts, caramel, and gum or any hard or crunchy food. Try not to eat them.

Remember, adequate and satisfactory oral hygiene is paramount for success with braces.

In general, you will find your life is largely unchanged with braces. Before long, you will not even think about your new routine.
