Dental Implants

The best way to replace missing teeth

Missing teeth are a painful, embarrassing, and traumatic condition. They can affect your self-esteem, job prospects, daily living and lead to a decline in quality of life.

You may experience difficulty in chewing, pain while eating or drinking, and possibly even speech problems.

Missing teeth can lead to a derangement of your bite, which causes food to get trapped between your teeth and excessive load on the existing teeth. This leads to tooth decay and gum disease, which can eventually result in losing more teeth.

The good news is that there is a solution.

Dental Implants are the only permanent solution that can replace a missing tooth or several teeth at once. They provide you with the same look and function as your natural teeth and allow you to eat, speak, and brush like normal so that you will be confident enough to show off your new smile!

What are Dental Implants?

A dental implant is a synthetic tooth root surgically placed in the bone to restore a missing tooth.... The implant is made up of 3 parts: the post, abutment, and porcelain crown. A titanium post or a screw is surgically placed in the bone below the gums to create a foundation for attaching a dental crown or prosthetic tooth. Once the implant has been firmly secured to the jaw, an abutment is fitted securely in place to complete this part of the treatment.

The titanium post becomes incorporated into your jawbone by a natural process called as osseintegration. This is one of the most significant advantages of dental implants and makes the implant firm and securely attached to the bone.

The crown part is typically done after 3 months of implant placement in the bone. This allows for achieving adequate stability.

Patients with implants need to be proactive in their maintenance protocol and will need regular checkups to ensure that the implant is functioning properly and that any complications, such as infections, loose crowns, etc are treated promptly.

The entire procedure, on average, takes three to six months. The majority of patients are not affected in their normal routine. The implant placement which is a surgical appointment will need about an hour or so and we at Amelia Street Dental pride ourselves in the absolute aseptic protocol followed. All our dentists and staff are highly trained for this treatment modality.

Simply put, dental implants can provide you with the healthy, natural-looking tooth that you deserve.

Why Might You Need A Dental Implant?

Dental implants are the best conservative treatment option for replacing missing ... teeth by fixed natural-looking and functioning artificial teeth.

In the past, patients only had the choice was to wear removable dentures or have fixed bridges placed alongside two adjacent teeth. This is still an advantageous and practical option, but implants do not require removable appliances or filing down adjacent teeth.

For some, the decision between dentures, bridges and dental implants are a difficult one. While dentures and bridges are both viable options for replacing missing teeth, with their own set of advantages, dental implants are often more popular due to the following reasons:

- Dental implants offer a permanent solution to tooth loss
- Unlike dentures, there won't be any slipping, clicking, or goopy pastes.
- Restoring chewing function and strength.
- Unlike bridges that need to be replaced every 10-15 years, dental implants last for a lifetime.
- Due to their design, dental implants have a greater functionality than bridges. They feel more like a natural tooth.
