White Fillings

Stop decay and enjoy a glowing white smile

Amelia Street Dental Clinic Offers White Fillings for a Polished Smile

How many fillings do you have? By the time we reach adulthood, most of us have at least a couple.

Chances are if you got those fillings years ago, they were made of metal or amalgam alloy. Needless to say, metal fillings can mar an otherwise white and shining smile.

At Amelia Street Dental Clinic, we offer white composite fillings.The next time you need to have a cavity filled, you can choose this option for a flawless finish. No one will be able to see you had a filling—maybe not even you!

Say Goodbye to Old-Fashioned Fillings

A tooth filling shouldn’t be an eyesore. And when you choose a white filling, it isn’t. ... Our state-of-the-art white composite material looks so much like tooth enamel that afterwards, you could easily forget you ever had a cavity.

Are White Fillings Right For You?

White fillings are an excellent choice for just about anyone.... We recommend them if you want a natural, seamless look.

Why Choose White Fillings?

• Your fillings will blend with your enamel, and other tooth stucture so you won’t even know they are there. ...
• Unlike metal fillings, composite fillings do not conduct heat, keeping tooth sensitivity to a minimum.
• Composite fillings are stable as temperatures rise and fall, unlike metal fillings. That means that they do not put strain on your teeth by expanding or contracting.
• Some people worry about the safety of metal fillings and may feel safer with composite fillings.
• White composite fillings are bonded to the teeth so they offer better retention and preserve the remaining natural tooth structure.
• There are number of tooth-coloured shades available in composite filling materials so this allows for exact shade matching.
• It is economical and hence the most widely used restorative material.

How We Stop Decay and Brighten Your Smile

Getting white fillings is just as fast and simple a process as getting metal fillings. ...

1. We apply a topical anesthetic when you come in for your appointment.
2. We check with you to make sure that the area is completely numb.
3. A gentle dental expert will drill away the parts of your tooth that are decayed.
4. Next, we clean the tooth and pre-treat it specific agents to ensure adequate bonding.
5. Then, we fill the tooth and adapt it in the cavity and finally cure it to harden it. A special blue light is used for this purpose.
6. The last step is to check your bite and to polish the filling. Now, it looks just like part of your tooth!

You will usually be done getting a single filling in less than 20 minutes. If you need more than one filling, it may take more time.

How to Maintain Your White Fillings

Taking care of your white fillings involves the same routines as preventing the need ... for future fillings. Brush, floss, and use mouthwash each day to protect your teeth, fillings and gums from bacteria and plaque.

We can offer you additional instructions on oral hygiene during your visit.

Amelia Street Dental Clinic’s white composite fillings look almost identical to enamel and your natural tooth and are tough enough to last for decades.
