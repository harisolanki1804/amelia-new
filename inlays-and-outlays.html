Inlays and Onlays

Restore Damaged Teeth to Full Strength and Function with Inlays and Onlays

Are some of your teeth weakened because of tooth decay? Or have large fillings which are constantly chipping off? Do you miss the days when chewing your food was easy?

Amelia Street Dental Clinic can strengthen your teeth and protect them from further decay with inlays or onlays.

What are Dental Inlays and Onlays?

Inlays and onlays are best described as conservative alternatives to traditional ... crowns and fillings.

Like fillings and crowns, they are appropriate for repairing teeth that have degraded through tooth decay.

Are You a Candidate for Inlays or Onlays?

You may be a candidate for inlays or onlays if you have dental decay which is... extensive or major part of the tooth has chipped off.

We turn to inlays and onlays when we need to reinforce the tooth and provide more strength than a basic composite filling.

We will choose an inlay if the tooth decay is located between cusps. If, on the other hand, the cusps themselves are affected, we will opt for an onlay.

Benefits of Inlays or Onlays

• Cover a wider area than a traditional filling allows....
• Avoid a more invasive procedure like putting on a crown.
• The durability of inlays and onlays is more means they can last for decades.
• Chew your food with ease once you have repaired your teeth.
• Your teeth are strengthened by the inlays or onlays, protecting them in the future. In fact, inlays and onlays can strengthen your teeth more than traditional fillings.
• It may cost less to get an inlay or onlay than it would to get a crown.
• As it is fabricated in the dental laboratory, it offers precision fit, high aesthetics and great strength.

Nowadays, inlays and onlays are fabricated in metal free ceramics are considered the gold standard in restorative dentistry.

What to Expect When Getting Dental Inlays or Onlays

1. Once you have discussed the pros and cons of the inlay or onlay treatment option and a decision ... to make an inlay or onlay is consented to, our dental experts will apply a local anesthetic, and then use a drill to remove the decayed portion of the tooth and shape the tooth to its desired form. All aspects of form, function and aesthetics are determined at this appointment.

2. At that same appointment, we will create an impression of the tooth or intraoral scan of the prepared tooth

3. We will insert a provisional restoration in the prepared tooth to protect them and enable you to chew and eat properly. You can then head home and wait for your next appointment.

4. Our experienced local lab specialists based in Perth will create your custom inlay or onlay.

5. You come in for your second appointment, where we take off the temporary restoration. We then fit you with your permanent inlay or onlay, making adjustments as needed until we have achieved the ideal fit. This appointment may also need local anesthesia.

How to Care for Your Dental Inlays or Onlays

Caring for your inlays or onlays is easy—just brush, floss, and use mouthwash as you normally do to take care of your oral hygiene.
